The Problem

Spark's Performance Ceiling

Spark Is the Default — But Not Free

30M+ Monthly Maven Downloads
1,000+ Companies in Production
2,000+ Community Contributors

Yet many teams hit a CPU and cost wall: clusters scale out, bills go up, SLAs barely move.

If you've ever watched a Spark job crawl while your CPUs sit at 40% utilization, you've felt this pain.

The JVM Hits the Wall

Whole-Stage CodeGen (Spark 2.0) gave ~2× — but gains plateaued.

JVM LimitationImpact
🚫 No SIMD VectorizationModern CPUs process 8–16 values/instruction — JVM can't use this
⏸️ GC Pauses10–100ms pauses at scale = wasted time
🔗 Memory IndirectionObject headers, pointer chasing = cache misses
📦 Serialization OverheadData conversion between internal formats

Native engines like ClickHouse and Velox leave the JVM in the dust — 2× to 10× faster on the same hardware.

The Trade-Off Dilemma

🟠 Apache Spark — Scalability, Ecosystem, APIs (JVM-based)
⬍   Gap: Can we bridge this?   ⬍
🟢 Native SQL Engines — Raw Performance (Velox, ClickHouse)
Can we keep Spark's ecosystem while running like a native engine — without rewriting a single query?

Apache Gluten is our "yes" to that question.

Lessons Learned — The Hard Parts

Graduating in 2 years sounds fast — but it didn't feel fast while we were in it.

Lesson 1: Governance Is Harder Than Code

Early on, 90% of contributions came from Intel. ASF mentors were clear: "Diversify your PMC, or you won't graduate." We learned to make all decisions public, rotate meeting times globally, and explicitly invite dissent.

Lesson 2: Two Backends = Twice the Complexity

Supporting both Velox and ClickHouse nearly fractured the community. Solution: Backend abstraction layer — unified Spark integration, separated backend code.

More Lessons Learned

Lesson 3: Memory Management — The 9-Month Battle

Spark's on-heap vs. Velox's off-heap competed for RAM. We chose dynamic negotiation over fixed partitions. Took 9 months. OOM issues dropped 70% after 1.3.0.

Lesson 4: Stars ≠ Contributors

Conference talks got us GitHub stars, but stars don't write code. GlutenCon workshops with 80 hands-on attendees yielded 12 new active contributors in 2 months.

Lesson 5: IP Compliance Is a Blocker

A trademark concern surfaced the week before our graduation vote. 72 hours of legal research. Lesson: Do IP due diligence early.
